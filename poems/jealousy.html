Jealousy

Exploring the complex emotions of desire and envy.

Confusion, As I never wanted to see— Thinking, Felt to never think again. Scenes never seemed so beautiful, Yet never enjoyed again. Hurt. Am I hurt that you didn't care? Am I hurt that you didn’t feel? Am I hurt that you didn’t see The feeling coming from me? It feels like a wind breeze, A cold stare in colder weather, A death that embraced me With a frozen hug— A hug I never wanted to let go. In a dimension of different worlds, My friend stared at me from far ago. Why do I sense anger in your eyes? Is it illusion? Or a misunderstanding I never let go? The moments I gave you together— Were they not enough? The things I gave you— Were they not enough? I only wanted to be a small part Of a world that belongs to someone. The sky was clear— But I saw darkness hiding in it. Pitch-black eyes looked at me, Wanting me to think and sink. The relaxing warmth someone gave Turned into a wound that won’t heal. That day I received a call— A voice so sweet, I didn’t like mine. I should’ve been ashamed— Because I never should have left. Wanting to follow, see, talk, enjoy the moments With someone was the thing I enjoyed. But was that all there was? Was that the day I saw two things in the same place— One side warm, showing love, The other cold, hiding anger? It wanted to devour me whole. But then… I saw a light. A light, shining like a star— Bright and warm, Yet not a place I could reach. So close… Yet so far. Admiring from a distance Was all I had. A star, surrounded by stars, Shining so bright— I felt dimmed. Waiting To be extinguished and disappear, Not knowing anything at all. Curiosity searched for answers I never had. Maybe deep down, I didn’t want them. Afraid the stage I built Would fall apart. My friend stared at me— Intensely, Maybe with a hidden rage. Comparison had rooted itself In a heart that wouldn’t let go. You tried to distract me, A trick you learned to hide the truth, With a show. Too bad for you, That was just a joke. That side glance you gave her— I saw it. A feeling I didn’t want to see. A flower has many bees— You were just one of these. As I searched for that friend inside me, Many misunderstood that friend To be something else for me. Maybe it was inner me. Or imaginary. Jealousy was never a part of me— But as I continued to see, I realized I did feel Somewhat Lonely.
- Gaurab Aryal
